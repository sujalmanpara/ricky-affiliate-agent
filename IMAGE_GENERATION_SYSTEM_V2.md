# Image Generation System V2 - Research-Backed Design Principles

## 🎯 What Makes Viral Affiliate Marketing Banners Work

Based on analysis of high-performing marketing (Skechers example, Nike, Apple, modern e-commerce), here's what separates scroll-stopping banners from "dumb" ones:

---

## ❌ Why Most AI-Generated Banners Look "Dumb"

### Problem 1: No Visual Hierarchy
- Everything competes for attention equally
- Product, price, discount, features all fighting
- Eye doesn't know where to look first
- Result: Cognitive overload → scroll past

### Problem 2: No Psychological Triggers
- Just product + price (functional only)
- No emotion, no story, no aspiration
- Doesn't answer: "Why should I care?"
- Result: No emotional connection → no click

### Problem 3: No Platform Awareness
- Same bland layout for Twitter, Instagram, Pinterest
- Doesn't respect platform's native visual language
- Looks like an ad (people trained to ignore ads)
- Result: Feels foreign → gets skipped

---

## ✅ The Research-Backed Solution

### 1. VISUAL HIERARCHY (The 3-Second Rule)

**What viewers see in order:**
1. **0-1 sec:** Dominant visual element (product or headline)
2. **1-2 sec:** Secondary info (price or benefit)
3. **2-3 sec:** Supporting details (features, CTA)

**How to implement:**
- **Size hierarchy:** Biggest element = most important
- **Contrast hierarchy:** Highest contrast = grabs attention first
- **Position hierarchy:** Top-left or center = viewed first

**Example from Skechers:**
1. First: Bold headline "ULTRA COMFORT. EFFORTLESS STYLE."
2. Second: The shoe (center, with yellow insole pop)
3. Third: Product name and CTA

### 2. PSYCHOLOGICAL TRIGGERS (Why People Buy)

**The 7 Core Triggers:**

| Trigger | What It Is | How to Use It |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| **Urgency** | Fear of missing out | "Limited time", "26% OFF", countdown |
| **Social Proof** | Others validate it | "★★★★★ (12,438 reviews)" |
| **Aspiration** | Desire to be better | Lifestyle context, "be like this" |
| **Problem-Solution** | Pain point relief | Before/after, "Never X again" |
| **Exclusivity** | Feel special | "Members only", "First 100" |
| **Authority** | Expert endorsement | Brand logos, "Best of 2026" |
| **Simplicity** | Reduce friction | "Just ₹X", "One click away" |

**Critical: Use 2-3 triggers per banner, not all 7!**

### 3. PLATFORM NATIVE DESIGN (Respect the Medium)

**Twitter/X (16:9):**
- **Behavior:** Fast scrolling, text-heavy platform
- **Design:** Bold headline top-left, high contrast, punchy hook
- **Psychology:** Urgency + Social proof
- **Text:** Minimal, impactful (headline + price + CTA max)
- **Example:** "Stop Overpaying for X → ₹2,999 (was ₹4,999)"

**Instagram (1:1):**
- **Behavior:** Visual-first, lifestyle browsing
- **Design:** Clean, centered product, aspirational context
- **Psychology:** Aspiration + Simplicity
- **Text:** Subtle, let image speak (product name + price badge only)
- **Example:** Product in beautiful flat-lay with lifestyle items

**Pinterest (2:3):**
- **Behavior:** Research mode, saving for later
- **Design:** Info-rich, vertical story format, benefit-focused
- **Psychology:** Authority + Problem-solution
- **Text:** Title banner + feature list + social proof
- **Example:** "Best [Category] Under ₹X - Complete Guide"

## 🎨 The Complete Design System

### HEADLINE FORMULAS (Tested & Proven)

**Never describe the product. Trigger emotion instead.**

| Bad (Descriptive) | Good (Emotional) |
|-------------------|------------------|
| "Samsung 20000mAh Power Bank" | "Never Die Mid-Day Again" |
| "Zebronics Wireless Keyboard" | "Cut the Cable Chaos" |
| "Samsung 183L Refrigerator" | "Fresh Food. Happy Family." |
| "Nike Running Shoes" | "Run Faster. Feel Lighter." |

**The 4 Headline Patterns:**

1. **Problem Solve:** "Stop/Never/End [Pain Point]"
   - "Stop Wasting Money on [X]"
   - "Never Run Out of Battery Again"

2. **Aspiration:** "Be/Feel/Get [Desired State]"
   - "Feel Like a Pro"
   - "Get the Kitchen You Deserve"

3. **Comparison:** "[This] Not [That]"
   - "Smart Storage. Not Clutter."
   - "Fresh Food. Not Waste."

4. **Command:** "[Action Verb] [Benefit]"
   - "Upgrade Your Workspace"
   - "Transform Your Kitchen"

### COLOR PSYCHOLOGY BY CATEGORY

| Category | Palette | Why |
|----------|---------|-----|
| **Tech Gadgets** | Dark luxury (black, silver, blue) | Premium, modern, sophisticated |
| **Kitchen/Home** | Fresh green + warm wood | Natural, healthy, family |
| **Fashion** | Dark luxury or bold accent | Style, confidence, elegance |
| **Fitness** | Cool premium (blue, white, energy accent) | Clean, energetic, performance |
| **Budget Deals** | Warm urgency (red, orange, yellow) | Excitement, action, limited time |

### TEXT OVERLAY RULES

**The 3-Element Maximum:**
Never put more than 3 text elements on one image!

**Twitter/X:**
1. Headline (top)
2. Price badge (corner)
3. Discount badge (opposite corner)

**Instagram:**
1. Product name (subtle, bottom)
2. Price badge (small, corner)
3. (Optional: Brand logo)

**Pinterest:**
1. Title banner (top)
2. Price + discount (on product area)
3. Feature list (bottom section)

### NEGATIVE SPACE REQUIREMENT

**30-40% of canvas must be empty!**

Why:
- Gives eye a place to rest
- Makes key elements pop
- Looks premium (not cluttered)
- Better mobile readability

**Bad:** Product + text + badges + background items + effects = chaos  
**Good:** Product + 1 headline + 1 price badge + lots of breathing room

### CONTRAST RULES

**High contrast = attention!**

| Background | Text/Badge Color |
|------------|------------------|
| **Dark** (black, navy, dark green) | White, bright yellow, red |
| **Light** (white, cream, light gray) | Black, navy, deep red |
| **Medium** (wood, stone, gray) | White OR black (test both) |

**The Pop Test:**
Squint at your banner. What stands out?
- Should be: Product + Headline + Price
- If something else pops first: reduce its contrast

## 🚀 The Two-Tier Strategy

### TIER 1: Simple Text Overlays (Fast & Free)

**When to use:**
- Daily 3 products
- Quick value communication
- Budget-conscious users
- Consistent posting rhythm

**Process:**
1. Use Amazon's professional photo
2. Add 3 text elements max (headline, price, discount)
3. Apply platform-specific placement
4. Use category-appropriate colors

**Time:** 3 seconds  
**Cost:** $0  
**Quality:** 10/10 product accuracy, functional appeal

### TIER 2: Creative Lifestyle (Nano Banana Pro)

**When to use:**
- High-value products (₹5,000+)
- Campaign launches
- Special promotions
- Premium brands

**Process:**
1. Use Amazon photo as reference
2. Generate creative lifestyle scene with Nano Banana Pro
3. Preserve exact product via img2img
4. Add sophisticated text overlays

**Time:** 70 seconds  
**Cost:** $0.30  
**Quality:** 10/10 product accuracy + Skechers-level creative

### USER CHOICE MODEL

**Budget Mode:**
- All Tier 1 (simple overlays)
- 90 products/month = $0
- Good for: Testing, high volume, tight budget

**Mixed Mode (RECOMMENDED):**
- 27 Tier 1 + 3 Tier 2 per week
- 80 simple + 10 creative per month = $3/month
- Good for: Smart spending, feature best products

**Premium Mode:**
- All Tier 2 (creative lifestyle)
- 90 products/month = $27/month
- Good for: Brand building, high-engagement focus

## 📐 Platform-Specific Templates

### TWITTER/X Template (16:9 - 1200×675px)

```
Layout:
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│ [BOLD HEADLINE TOP-LEFT]      [DISC]│  ← Discount badge
│                                 26%  │
│         [PRODUCT IMAGE]         OFF  │
│           (centered)                 │
│                                      │
│ [₹PRICE]                             │  ← Price badge bottom-left
└─────────────────────────────────────┘

Elements:
- Background: Dark overlay (0.3 opacity) over Amazon photo
- Headline: Bold, white, 48-60pt, top-left
- Product: Centered, 60% of canvas
- Price badge: Bottom-left, orange (#FF6B35)
- Discount badge: Top-right, red circle
- Negative space: 35%

Colors:
- Tech: Dark bg + white text + blue accent
- Home: Light bg + dark text + green accent
- Fashion: Dark bg + gold accent
```

### INSTAGRAM Template (1:1 - 1080×1080px)

```
Layout:
┌─────────────────────────┐
│                         │
│                         │
│    [PRODUCT IMAGE]      │  ← Centered, hero focus
│      (centered)         │
│                         │
│                         │
│              [₹PRICE]   │  ← Small badge bottom-right
└─────────────────────────┘

Elements:
- Background: Clean white OR lifestyle blur
- Product: Centered, 50-60% of canvas
- Text: Minimal! Product name subtle at bottom
- Price badge: Small, bottom-right corner
- Negative space: 40%

Style:
- Clean, aspirational, let product shine
- Optional: Flat-lay context items (subtle)
- Mood: "This could be mine"
```

### PINTEREST Template (2:3 - 1000×1500px)

```
Layout:
┌───────────────────────┐
│ [TITLE BANNER TOP]    │  ← Green banner 100px
│ "Best X Under ₹Y"     │
├───────────────────────┤
│                       │
│  [PRODUCT IMAGE]      │
│    + 6 badges         │  ← Star, capacity, price, etc.
│  (centered 50%)       │
│                       │
├───────────────────────┤
│ Why Choose This?      │  ← Dark gray footer 250px
│ ✓ Feature 1           │
│ ✓ Feature 2           │
│ ✓ Feature 3           │
│ "Complete guide ↓"    │
└───────────────────────┘

Elements:
- Top banner: Category color, 100px, bold title
- Product: Center 50%, with floating badge icons
- Bottom section: Dark bg, white text, feature list
- Negative space: 30%

Info density:
- More text OK (Pinterest = research mode)
- Include: Rating, capacity, warranty, energy star
- Make it "pin-worthy" (save for later value)
```

## 🧪 A/B Testing Framework

**Test variables (one at a time!):**

1. **Headline style:** Problem vs Aspiration vs Command
2. **Color palette:** Dark luxury vs Fresh green vs Warm urgency
3. **Badge placement:** Top-right vs Bottom-left for discount
4. **Text amount:** Minimal (Instagram) vs Medium (Twitter) vs Rich (Pinterest)
5. **Background:** Clean white vs Dark overlay vs Lifestyle blur

**Success metrics:**
- Click-through rate (CTR)
- Engagement rate (likes, shares)
- Conversion rate (actual sales)

**Run test:** 2 versions, 50 posts each, track for 1 week

## 💡 Pro Tips from the Skechers Analysis

1. **The Yellow Insole Trick:**
   - In a dark image, ONE bright accent color = eye magnet
   - Use this: Highlight product's KEY feature with color pop
   - Example: Yellow insole, red discount badge, green energy star

2. **The Flat-Lay Psychology:**
   - Surrounding lifestyle items = "this fits my life"
   - Creates aspirational identity, not just product
   - Use sparingly (high-value products only, costs $0.30)

3. **The 3D Text Effect:**
   - Subtle bevel/shadow makes text "physical"
   - Feels premium, not flat/cheap
   - PIL can do this: `ImageFilter.EMBOSS` or manual shadow layer

4. **The Colored Light Glow:**
   - Subtle colored light behind product = "tech innovation"
   - Blue = cooling, freshness, technology
   - Red/orange = warmth, energy, excitement
   - PIL: Gaussian blur + color overlay + blend mode

5. **The Brand Trademark Symbols:**
   - ®, ™ symbols = trust, legitimacy, established brand
   - Even if not your brand, shows you respect IP
   - Adds professional polish
